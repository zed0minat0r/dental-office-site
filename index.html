Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before your visit. If you do not see your question here, give us a call.

What should I bring to my first appointment?

Please bring your photo ID, insurance card, and a list of any medications you are currently taking. If you have recent dental X-rays from a previous provider, those are helpful too. We will have some paperwork for you to fill out, so arriving 15 minutes early is a good idea.

Do you accept my insurance?

We accept most major dental insurance plans including Delta Dental, Cigna, Aetna, MetLife, Guardian, UnitedHealthcare, Humana, and BlueCross BlueShield. If you do not see your provider listed, please call us — we are happy to verify your coverage before your visit.

What if I am anxious about dental visits?

You are not alone — dental anxiety is very common, and we take it seriously. We offer a full comfort menu including noise-canceling headphones, weighted blankets, ceiling TVs, and sedation options ranging from laughing gas to oral sedation. Our team will go at your pace and you can raise your hand to pause at any time.

How much does a first visit cost without insurance?

We currently offer a new patient special of $99 for a comprehensive exam and digital X-rays. For patients without insurance, we also offer our Bright Smile Savings Plan with discounted rates on all services. We accept CareCredit and offer flexible payment plans as well.

Do you see children? What age should my child first visit?

Absolutely. Dr. Maria Santos is our pediatric dentist and she is wonderful with kids of all ages. The American Dental Association recommends a first visit by age 1 or within 6 months of the first tooth appearing. We make visits fun and positive so your child builds healthy habits early.

What should I do in a dental emergency?

Call our emergency line at (555) 987-6543 right away. We offer same-day emergency appointments for toothaches, broken or knocked-out teeth, and other urgent issues. If a tooth is knocked out, keep it moist (in milk or saliva) and get to us as quickly as possible.
